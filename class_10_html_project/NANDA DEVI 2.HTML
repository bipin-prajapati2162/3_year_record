Nanda Devi

Nanda Devi is the second largest mountain in India with elevation of 25,643 feet.
Nanda Devi Mountain is located in the state of Uttarakhand in India, and it is part of the Garhwal Himalayas.
The Locals beliefs that the mountain is home to Parvati the goddess. The Nanda Devi National Park was founded in 1982 to protect the flora and fauna, and was subsequently named a World Heritage site by UNESCO.
